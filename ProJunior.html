MUSIC CLASSES FOR KIDS - PRO JUNIOR COURSE

The Pro Junior course is perfectly tailored for youngsters who are interested in an active, engaging and in-depth experience of music. Students who are inclined towards pursuing their musical education beyond the satisfaction of just a hobby in piano, guitar or singing, will be taught comprehensive theory and practical knowledge through this age-appropriate, systematic course. For this purpose, they will have to undergo an assessment, following which they could be selected for this exclusive programme. The course is split across three levels, each consisting subjects such as theory and ear training, instrument and ensemble.We offer lessons in piano, guitar, bass, drums and western vocals.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of Level 1 a successful student should be able to:
1. Display a high level of fluency with the use of a DAW, having a basic ability to edit, record, synthesise, sequence, understand signal flow, mix projects and use sampling.
2. Develop a basic understanding of the physics of recording in order to explain why certain techniques are used over others.
3. Identify broad genre definitions and compose to those genres.
4.Give constructive peer feedback and verbalise their own ideas in order to pitch them to others. A level of competency in computers.
Students will be given the opportunity to learn production techniques that are used by producers like Trent Reznor, Timbaland, Max Martin and RedOne. They will have had the opportunity to produce ten pieces in a range of production styles using various techniques.

THEORY
THEORY

Contents
This module lays the foundation of musical understanding and will across the three levels, grow in depth. From learning to read music and understand chord progressions, to being able to differentiate between minor and major triads, the student will learn to “experience” the music and understand what goes into making it.

Outcomes:


By the end of the three levels, the student will be able to read musical notation, know to use polyphonic writing, identify complex rhythms, and differentiate between various types of 7th chords, among others.

FACULTY

SAURAV GHOSH

Saurav has been playing drums since he was 15 years old and is a native of Calcutta, India. Having performed with numerous local acts during his teenage years, he decided to study music formally. While attaining a Foundation Certificate in Music Theory at the International College of Music, Kuala Lumpur he discovered his love for Jazz music and this lead him to further pursue his studies at the London Centre of Contemporary Music, UK in 2011.
Under the guidance of prolific British musicians such as Pete Cater, Richard Brook, Dave O’Higgins and Simon Wallace among others, he gained valuable knowledge in Performance, Composition and Production in Jazz and other contemporary styles of music. Saurav spent three years in the heart of London’s music scene and has performed at multiple venues such as the O2 Academy and the Wilton’s Music Hall with acts such as singer-songwriter Greyce and various LCCM ensembles. He also had the good fortune of being able to attend master classes by world-renowned musicians such as Michel Camilo and Benny Greb during his time in London.
